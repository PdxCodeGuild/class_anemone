Who is Huey Long?

Huey Long was a Louisiana politician known as "the Kingfish" after a character in an Amos n' Andy character. Long was born on August 30, 1883, near Winnfield Lousiiana. Winnfield was an incredibly impoverished parish in Louisiana and Mr. Long grew up poor. He was one of 9 children, and was homeschooled by his family until he turned 11. Ultimately Mr. Long would attend Oklahoma Baptist University, as well as other schools, and would eventually be admitted as an attorney to the Lousisiana State Bar.

What did Huey Long do?

Huey Long has a number of notable acccomplishments, chief of which was getting elected as Governor of the State of Louisana in 1928, and later getting elected as a senator for Louisiana in 1930. Mr. Long was a southern-democrat, but was known for being regarded as, what was at the time considered, a racial liberal. His policies and politics were intended to improve the lot of all poor people in Louisiana, and he sought to do so by taking on the various oil and gas interests that dominated the state's politis at the time.
